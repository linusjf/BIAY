The Kingdom of God, which was destroyed in the Garden of Eden, will be restored by beginning with evangelisation.

The physical Body of Christ was present in limited space and time in the first century in Israel but the ascended mystical Body of Christ can be present in multiple places at the same time and His Body includes all His flock.
The disciples wanted to know when the good times would come when they would be freed from their enemies and they would see peace in their land.
Like the disciples, sometimes we too ask the same question because we want to be free from our trouble-making enemy (Satan) and enjoy peace in our lives.
But Jesus reminds us that our duty is not to focus on the turmoil or things of this world.  Our duty is to get busy and start evangelizing the world so that Jesus can use us to bring maximum people into His Kingdom and thus bring peace in our lives.

The Kingdom of God would start in Israel but would not be limited to the territory of Israel.  It would spread out to the whole world.
It would be an invisible kingdom made up of invisible souls.

Romans 1:3
Jesus is the Son of God, descended from David according to the flesh.
Jesus redeemed the world by offering His flesh and blood in atonement for our sins, the same flesh and blood which He received from his Mother Mary.

In the Old Covenant, every first born male was pre-destined by his first born status to serve as a common priest, by offering the daily sacrifices to God, and protecting His family from evil, even to the point of death.

Jesus, the first born of Mary by flesh, has fulfilled His duty as our older brother, by becoming the sacrificial priest and protecting His siblings from the enemy (Satan). Therefore, through baptism all Christians, who obey Godâ€™s commandments, become the second born of Mary (Revelation 12:17) and thus belong to God's family.\
If Jesus is our older brother then Mary is our Mother, and that is why Jesus gave her to us (His beloved disciples) (John 19:26-27).

If Jesus is our Bridegroom then, by the law, Mary is still our Mother.
In the earthly Davidic kingdom, as the son of Joseph, Jesus was the rightful heir to the earthly throne after the death of Joseph.  Therefore, by the law, Mary was the rightful Queen Mother according to the flesh.

Likewise, in the heavenly Davidic kingdom, since Jesus is our King, Mary becomes our rightful heavenly Queen Mother according to the flesh.
